**Today's rule of the day:** *Today there is no puzzle. Today you play CHESS — the whole board, all the rules, beginning to end.* Win, lose, or tie: finish a real game and you are, officially and forever, a chess player.

**Goal for today:** by the end, your child has played one complete legal game — set up the board themselves, castled if they could, asked the danger question on their own, and shaken hands at the end.

## Part 2 — The three opening habits (4 min) 🌱

Grown-up players start every game the same three ways. Say them once, then let the game happen — habits grow from playing, not lectures:

1. **Wake up the horses and bishops first.** (Knights and bishops out into the middle. Give every soldier a turn before anyone goes twice.)
2. **Castle early.** (Day 11's teleport — get the king behind his pawn wall before the fighting starts.)
3. **Don't send the queen out first.** (She's too precious — early queens get chased around by little pieces having a wonderful time.)

---

## Part 3 — The game (15 min) ⚔️

Play Pip's way, strictly — announce his law before move one: **"I take the biggest free thing. If nothing's free, I make a simple developing move."** Don't set traps, don't play brilliantly. Be a hungry, honest goblin. Pip punishes exactly one thing — forgetfulness — which is exactly what a first game should punish.

House rules for game one:

- **The danger question is legal to say out loud.** Before each of their moves: *"anybody in danger?"* Soon it goes silent and internal — today it's a spoken ritual.
- **One take-back each.** Even grandmasters wince at their own moves. One royal pardon per side, spent whenever they like.
- **All checks get announced** — "check!" is the best word in the game; let them shout it.
- **Play to the actual end.** Checkmate (day 10!), stalemate (the sneaky tie!), or a handshake draw if both armies are down to bare kings. No abandoning ship. Finishing IS the achievement.

If the game ends fast — someone's queen wandered off and got munched, games do that — shake hands, swap colors, play again. Two short games beat one long lecture.

---

## Wrap-up — the handshake (2 min) 🤝

Whatever happened — mate, tie, or a glorious mess — the ritual is the same: shake hands and each say ONE favorite move you made. If they lost: *"Pip got you today. What will he NOT get tomorrow?"* Losing to a goblin, then out-thinking him next game, is the entire engine of chess improvement — it starts tonight.

And say the big thing plainly, because it's true: **"You just played a whole game of chess. You're a chess player now. Nobody can ever take that back."**
