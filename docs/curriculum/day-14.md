# Chess — Day 14: Graduation Day 🎓

**For:** a brand-new chess player (age 7) · **Time:** ~25 min, all celebration · **You need:** a real board + pieces, and something that can be a trophy

> **Status: design spec.** The app can't serve this day yet — it reuses
> days 8–13's machinery (review serving + Pip's game mode); it needs no
> new seams of its own. Everything below runs today on a real board.

**Today's rule of the day:** *There is no new rule. You know them all.* Fourteen days ago the board was sixty-four mystery squares. Today a whole army answers to you. Today we prove it, celebrate it, and peek at the road ahead.

**Goal for today:** your child runs the victory lap (teaching YOU), wins one last staged mate, plays the graduation game — and hears, clearly, that this is a beginning, not an ending.

---

## Part 1 — The victory lap: they teach, you learn (8 min) 🎤

Swap chairs — today they hold the teacher's chalk. You play the confused student (badly, and with feeling). One question per day; let them explain each with pieces on the board:

1. Which way do the ranks and files run? *(Day 1)*
2. Show me the rook's plus and the bishop's X. Why can't this bishop ever visit that white square? *(Days 1–2)*
3. Is the queen really a rook and bishop glued together? Prove it. *(Day 3)*
4. Which piece is the ONLY jumper? *(Day 4)*
5. March a pawn to the end. What happens?! *(Day 5)*
6. Why does the king only take baby steps? *(Day 6)*
7. Two rooks or a queen — which is bigger? *(Day 7)*
8. Point at the piece in danger — how did you KNOW? *(Day 8)*
9. Someone shouts CHECK. Name the three doors. *(Day 9)*
10. Frozen but not in check — win or tie? *(Day 10)*
11. Do the teleport. Both directions. What are its laws? *(Day 11)*
12. What does Pip do to unguarded pieces? *(Day 12)*
13. What are the three opening habits? *(Day 13)*

Every confident answer is a skill that is now THEIRS. Miss one? Perfect — "teach it to me tomorrow" and move on. (In the app this lap is a mixed replay of favorite puzzles from all fourteen days.)

---

---

## Part 3 — The graduation game (10 min) ⚔️

One full game against Pip — the real ceremony. Same as Day 13, one change: **no spoken danger-question from you.** The training wheels come off; the question lives in their head now. Take-backs: still one each. Some traditions are sacred.

When it ends — however it ends — the handshake, and the diploma speech, one line, look them in the eye: **"Fourteen days. Six pieces. Every rule. You are a chess player."** Then the trophy: a captured pawn on a jar lid is a perfectly serious trophy. It goes on the shelf.

---

## Wrap-up — the doorway (3 min) 🚪

Tell them what's on the other side, because the eyes-going-wide is the point:

- **Next adventure — WINNING STUFF.** There's a move where one piece attacks TWO enemies at once, and no matter what your opponent does, they lose one. It's called a **fork**, it feels like a magic trick, and it's the first move that wins games on purpose.
- **Pip has cousins.** Braver ones. One of them plans ahead. You'll meet them when you're ready.
- **And someday** — checkmating with just a rook and king, pawn races, whole tournaments. The game never runs out of doors.

The lap is done; the spiral keeps climbing. Same board, deeper magic, for as long as it stays fun.

**Legend:** `K`/`Q` = your pieces · `k` = Pip's cornered king · 🏆 = one captured pawn, glued to a jar lid, absolutely priceless

**There is no "next time" teaser today.** Today just gets the trophy shelf, and the sentence that is now true forever: *she plays chess.*
