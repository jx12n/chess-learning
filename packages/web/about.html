♜ Niboo Learns Chess

One of one chess school

Niboo Learns Chess started as a simple idea: teach chess in a way that repeats, adapts, and can grow into more, one small lesson at a time.

One idea a day

Each day is a handful of tiny puzzles about exactly one thing. Practice until it clicks, then one checkpoint, and every day ends on purpose, while it's still fun. Grown-ups get a coach's script for each day, so someone who has never played chess can run the same lesson on a real board at the kitchen table.

    The first week is the first lap

    The days above teach the alphabet: the board and every piece. They are the start of a much longer road: check and checkmate (how games are actually won) come next, and beyond them tactics, endgames, and whole games against an opponent that plays like a person. Chess never runs out of next things to learn, and the school is built to keep pace, with every new chapter arriving through the same proof machine as the first seven days.

    No streaks, no daily nags, no finish line to race toward. Every session ends the same way: on purpose, while it's still fun.

    The honest machine

    Under the lessons sits a deterministic chess engine. Before any puzzle reaches a player, a verifier must prove it is solvable, and exactly as hard as its prompt promises. Anything unproven is refused, never quietly patched up. The same engine judges every click, so nothing on the board is ever a matter of opinion.

    The kind words are for the thinking; the chess facts come from the machine. That split is the whole design.

    Private by design

    No accounts, no ads, no tracking. Each player picks a name and a piece, and their progress lives in this browser and nowhere else. Clearing your browser data erases it, and that's the deal.
